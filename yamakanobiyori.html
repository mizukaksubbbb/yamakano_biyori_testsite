かつて富士吉田の街のいたるところに響いていた機織りの音。
それは、単なる産業の音ではなく、
人々の暮らしのリズムであり、地域の　時間　そのものでした。
織物産業が栄える上で重要な自然の音、
戦後産業を象徴する織機の音、
様々な音が交差する地点で始まる音楽祭。
この音楽祭は「音」を媒介に「地域」を体験する場に、
みんなでしていく場所です。

なりゆき。

/ CONCEPT / EN
The sound of looms once echoed throughout Fujiyoshida. It was not merely the sound of industry, but the rhythm of daily life—time itself in the region. A festival begins at the intersection of many sounds: the natural sounds essential to the growth of textile culture, the looms that symbolized postwar industry, and everything in between. This festival is a place we build together—where we experience the region through sound.

“Hiyori” means the kind of weather that feels just right for doing something—and also, letting things unfold. It carries the sense of spring’s first stirrings. What happens when people, music, and the history of Yamakano meet? We entrust ourselves to the air of the day, and to the way things naturally take shape.
